Venue: VMCC

IIT Bombay, Mumbai

ABOUT THE WORKSHOP

The Centre for Technology Alternatives for Rural Areas (CTARA) at IIT Bombay, in collaboration with experts from the University of Vermont (UofV), Burlington, USA, and University of Toronto (UofT), Canada, is arranging a roundtable meet cum workshop on “Challenges and Trends in Water Quality Monitoring of Rivers” in February 2024. The workshop is being organized as part of ‘Seed funding for Collaboration and Partnership Projects (SCPP)’ supported by the Institute of Eminence (IOE) funds of IIT Bombay and the agenda is to bring the various stakeholders working towards protecting water quality of surface water bodies on a common platform to share their knowledge and challenges to help build a comprehensive water quality plan (including sampling and testing) for surface water bodies to protect the water quality. As the main stakeholders include the State Pollution Control Boards, State Water Supply and Sanitation Departments, as well as academicians, we request you to deputy some of your regional officers, senior scientific / scientific officers for the workshop. The expected outcomes of this roundtable meeting are knowledge sharing between academicians and practitioners and bringing the discussion forward through collaboration while sharing challenges across various fields and understand the current trends in tackling these. The workshop will be followed by a field demonstration of new technologies developed by multiple academic and non-academic institutions.

STAKEHOLDERS

ACADEMIC AND RESEARCH INSTITUTES

Academic and research institutions involved in the research and development of various technologies and processes to facilitate the water quality monitoring of surface water bodies. The developed products may be useful in field to collect the sample and in-situ and laboratory testing.

POLLUTION CONTROL BOARD

The main regulatory bodies such as pollution control boards are majorly involved in the monitoring of river water quality through the national and state water quality monitoring programs. Their roles and duties are majorly to design monitoring network, select parameters to test and frequency of the monitoring.

WATER RESOURCES DEPARTMENTS

The department of State Government is involved in maintaining the water resources and regulating its usage. These departments also maintain and monitors the data of different water bodies such as lakes, dams etc. The department with its hydrology department also involves in monitoring the flows and its quality.

RIVER MANAGEMENT AUTHORITIES

River management authorities are involved in the monitoring and protection of the river. These agencies monitor the data on rainfall, discharges, and its quality concerning suitability for various usages.

WATER SUPPLY AND SANITATION DEPARTMENT

Water supply and sanitation departments supply safe drinking water to urban and rural setups. To maintain safe drinking water through proper design of water treatment plants and identifying sources of water with adequate supply of non-contaminated water.

CONSULTANTS FOR RIVER ACTION PLANS

The consultants are involved in designing and preparing detailed project reports on river protection and action plans, water resource management and water supply schemes, and wastewater management.
